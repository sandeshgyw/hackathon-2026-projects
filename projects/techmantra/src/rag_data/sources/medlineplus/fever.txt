OVERVIEW:
A fever is a body temperature above the normal range, usually defined 
as 100.4F (38C) or higher. Fever is not a disease itself — it is a 
sign that your body is fighting an infection or illness.

TEMPERATURE RANGES:
- Normal: 97F to 99F (36.1C to 37.2C)
- Low grade fever: 99F to 100.4F (37.2C to 38C)
- Fever: 100.4F (38C) and above
- High fever: 103F (39.4C) and above
- Very high fever: 104F (40C) — seek medical care
- Dangerous fever: 106F (41.1C) or above — emergency

COMMON CAUSES:
- Viral infections: cold, flu, COVID-19, COVID variants
- Bacterial infections: strep throat, urinary tract infection, pneumonia
- Ear infections
- Sinus infections
- Heat exhaustion
- Some inflammatory conditions

ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS:
- Sweating and chills
- Headache
- Muscle aches
- Loss of appetite
- Dehydration
- General weakness and fatigue
- Sensitivity to light

WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE:
Adults — go to emergency room if:
- Fever above 103F (39.4C) that does not respond to medication
- Fever with stiff neck, severe headache, and sensitivity to light
- Fever with difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Fever with confusion or altered mental status
- Fever with severe abdominal pain
- Fever lasting more than 3 days

Infants — call doctor immediately if:
- Any fever in baby under 3 months
- Fever above 104F in older infants

HOME CARE FOR MILD FEVER:
- Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen as directed — do not give aspirin to children
- Drink plenty of fluids — water, broth, juice
- Rest and stay home
- Wear lightweight clothing
- Use a lukewarm (not cold) compress on forehead
- Keep room temperature comfortable

RISK TIER: LOW — mild fever can be managed at home.
MEDIUM — fever above 103F or lasting more than 3 days needs medical attention.
HIGH — fever with stiff neck, confusion, or difficulty breathing is an emergency.
