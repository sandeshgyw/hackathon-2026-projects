A headache is pain or discomfort in the head or face area. Types include tension headaches, cluster headaches, and migraines.

Tension headaches are the most common type. They cause mild to moderate pain and tend to come back on a regular basis. Causes include stress, poor posture, eye strain, and dehydration.

Self-care for tension headaches:
- Rest in a quiet, dark room
- Apply a warm or cold compress to your forehead or neck
- Stay hydrated — drink water throughout the day
- Take over-the-counter acetaminophen or ibuprofen as directed
- Practice relaxation techniques

Seek emergency care if your headache:
- Is sudden and severe (thunderclap headache)
- Comes with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or rash
- Follows a head injury
- Comes with vision changes, weakness, or difficulty speaking
