Source: MedlinePlus - Back Pain
URL: https://medlineplus.gov/backpain.html

OVERVIEW:
Back pain is one of the most common medical complaints. Most back pain 
improves within a few weeks with home treatment and is rarely serious. 
However some types of back pain require immediate medical attention.

TYPES:
Acute back pain: lasts a few days to a few weeks — most common
Chronic back pain: lasts more than 12 weeks
Lower back pain: most common location
Upper back pain: less common, can sometimes relate to heart or lung issues

COMMON CAUSES:
- Muscle or ligament strain from heavy lifting or awkward movement
- Bulging or ruptured disk pressing on a nerve
- Arthritis
- Osteoporosis
- Poor posture over time
- Sitting for long periods
- Kidney infection or kidney stones (pain in the back and sides)

SYMPTOMS:
- Muscle ache in the back
- Shooting or stabbing pain
- Pain that radiates down the leg (sciatica)
- Limited flexibility or range of motion
- Inability to stand straight

WARNING SIGNS REQUIRING EMERGENCY CARE:
Seek immediate care if back pain is accompanied by:
- Loss of bladder or bowel control (possible spinal cord compression — emergency)
- Numbness in the groin or inner thighs (saddle anesthesia)
- Fever and back pain together (possible spinal infection or kidney infection)
- Back pain after a fall or serious injury
- Severe pain that does not improve with rest
- Pain with weakness or numbness in both legs

UPPER BACK PAIN WARNING:
Upper back pain combined with chest pain, shortness of breath, or 
sweating may indicate a heart attack or aortic dissection — call 911.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Pain lasting more than a few weeks
- Pain that is severe and does not improve with rest
- Spreads down one or both legs especially below the knee
- Causes weakness, numbness, or tingling in legs
- Accompanied by unexplained weight loss

HOME CARE:
- Apply ice packs for the first 48 to 72 hours
- Switch to heat after 72 hours to relax muscles
- Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen for pain
- Stay as active as possible — avoid bed rest
- Gentle stretching and walking help recovery
- Avoid heavy lifting and twisting movements

RISK TIER: LOW — Most back pain resolves with home care within 4 weeks.
HIGH — Back pain with loss of bladder or bowel control is a spinal emergency. Go to ER immediately.
