OVERVIEW:
A sore throat is pain, scratchiness, or irritation of the throat that 
often worsens when you swallow. Sore throats are very common and most 
are caused by viral infections that resolve on their own.

SYMPTOMS:
- Pain or a scratchy sensation in the throat
- Pain that worsens with swallowing or talking
- Difficulty swallowing
- Sore or swollen glands in the neck or jaw
- Swollen, red tonsils
- White patches or pus on tonsils
- Hoarse or muffled voice
- Fever, cough, runny nose if caused by viral infection

COMMON CAUSES:
Viral infections (most common — 90% of cases):
- Common cold
- Flu (influenza)
- COVID-19
- Mononucleosis (mono)
- Measles

Bacterial infections:
- Strep throat — caused by Group A Streptococcus bacteria
- Requires antibiotic treatment to prevent complications
- Symptoms: sudden severe sore throat, fever, swollen lymph nodes, no cough

Other causes:
- Allergies — postnasal drip irritates the throat
- Dry air — especially in winter with heating systems
- Gastroesophageal reflux (GERD)
- Irritants: smoke, pollution, chemicals

STREP THROAT SIGNS:
- Sudden severe sore throat without cough or runny nose
- Fever above 101F
- White patches or streaks on the tonsils
- Swollen, tender lymph nodes in the neck
- Red spots on the roof of the mouth
- Rash (scarlet fever rash)

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Severe sore throat lasting more than a week
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing
- Fever above 101F
- Blood in saliva or phlegm
- Rash accompanying sore throat
- Hoarseness lasting more than 2 weeks
- Recurring sore throats

HOME CARE:
- Gargle with warm salt water several times a day
- Drink warm liquids: tea with honey, broth, warm water with lemon
- Use throat lozenges or sprays
- Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen for pain and fever
- Use a humidifier to add moisture to dry air
- Rest your voice
- Stay hydrated

RISK TIER: LOW — Most sore throats resolve in 5 to 7 days with home care.
See a doctor if you suspect strep throat — antibiotics are needed.
