OVERVIEW:
Dizziness is a common symptom that describes feelings of unsteadiness, 
lightheadedness, or vertigo (the sensation that you or the room is 
spinning). Most causes of dizziness are not serious but some require 
immediate medical attention.

TYPES OF DIZZINESS:
Vertigo — sensation of spinning or movement when still:
- Most common cause: BPPV (benign paroxysmal positional vertigo)
- Caused by inner ear problems
- Can also indicate stroke in some cases

Lightheadedness — feeling faint or about to pass out:
- Often caused by low blood pressure, dehydration, or standing up too fast
- Can be caused by heart problems

Disequilibrium — feeling unsteady or off balance:
- Often related to neurological conditions or inner ear problems

COMMON CAUSES:
- Dehydration or overheating
- Standing up too quickly (orthostatic hypotension)
- Inner ear problems: BPPV, labyrinthitis, Meniere's disease
- Anemia (low iron)
- Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)
- Medications: blood pressure drugs, sedatives, antidepressants
- Anxiety and panic attacks
- Migraine headaches

SERIOUS CAUSES — SEEK IMMEDIATE CARE:
- Stroke: sudden dizziness with facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- Heart attack: dizziness with chest pain, shortness of breath, sweating
- Severe dehydration
- Head injury
- Carbon monoxide poisoning

STROKE WARNING SIGNS — CALL 911:
Use FAST:
- Face drooping on one side
- Arm weakness — one arm drifts down when both raised
- Speech difficulty — slurred or strange speech
- Time to call 911

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Dizziness that is new, severe, or lasts longer than an hour
- Dizziness after a head injury
- Dizziness with fainting or loss of consciousness
- Dizziness with severe headache, vision changes, or hearing loss
- Dizziness with numbness or tingling
- Dizziness with irregular heartbeat

HOME CARE FOR MILD DIZZINESS:
- Sit or lie down immediately — do not drive
- Move slowly when changing positions
- Drink water if dehydrated
- Avoid caffeine, alcohol, salt, and tobacco
- Rest in a cool quiet place

RISK TIER: LOW — Mild dizziness often resolves with rest and hydration.
HIGH — Dizziness with facial drooping, arm weakness, or speech problems is a stroke emergency. Call 911.
