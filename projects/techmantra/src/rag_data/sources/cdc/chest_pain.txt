Source: CDC - Chest Pain and Heart Disease
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/heartdisease/symptoms.htm

OVERVIEW:
Chest pain is one of the most important symptoms in medicine. It can 
range from a minor muscle strain to a life-threatening heart attack or 
pulmonary embolism. Any new or unexplained chest pain should be 
evaluated by a medical professional immediately.

SYMPTOMS THAT INDICATE EMERGENCY:
- Chest pain, pressure, tightness, or squeezing sensation
- Pain spreading to the left arm, shoulder, neck, jaw, or back
- Shortness of breath with or without chest pain
- Cold sweat or clammy skin
- Nausea or vomiting
- Lightheadedness or sudden dizziness
- Heart racing or pounding (palpitations)
- Pain that comes on suddenly and is severe

TYPES OF CHEST PAIN:

Cardiac chest pain (heart-related — EMERGENCY):
- Feels like pressure, squeezing, fullness, or tightness
- May radiate to arm, neck, jaw, shoulder, or back
- Often accompanied by shortness of breath, sweating, nausea
- Can occur at rest or with minimal exertion

Pleuritic chest pain (lung-related):
- Sharp pain that worsens with deep breathing or coughing
- May indicate pleurisy, pneumonia, or pulmonary embolism
- Pulmonary embolism is a medical emergency

Musculoskeletal chest pain (less serious):
- Pain that worsens when you press on the chest wall
- Related to movement or specific positions
- Usually sharp and localized

WHEN TO CALL 911:
Call 911 immediately if chest pain:
- Comes on suddenly and is severe
- Is accompanied by shortness of breath, sweating, or nausea
- Radiates to the arm, jaw, or back
- Lasts more than a few minutes or goes away and comes back
- Occurs in a person with known heart disease or risk factors

Do not drive yourself. Do not wait to see if it gets better.

RISK FACTORS FOR CARDIAC CHEST PAIN:
- Age: Men over 45, women over 55
- Family history of heart disease
- High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes
- Smoking
- Obesity and physical inactivity

RISK TIER: HIGH — Chest pain requires immediate medical evaluation. 
Call 911 if pain is severe, sudden, or accompanied by other symptoms.
