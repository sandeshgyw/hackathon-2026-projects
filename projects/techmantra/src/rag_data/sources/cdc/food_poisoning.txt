Source: CDC - Food Poisoning Symptoms
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/symptoms.html

OVERVIEW:
Food poisoning results from eating food contaminated with bacteria, 
viruses, parasites, or toxins. Most cases resolve on their own within 
a few days. Some cases require medical attention.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea — may be bloody in serious cases
- Stomach cramps and abdominal pain
- Fever
- Headache
- Weakness and fatigue

ONSET TIMING:
Symptoms can start within hours or up to several days after eating 
contaminated food depending on the organism:
- 30 minutes to 6 hours: Staph aureus or Bacillus cereus
- 6 to 24 hours: Clostridium perfringens
- 1 to 3 days: Salmonella or E. coli
- 1 to 4 weeks: Listeria

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- High fever above 102F (39C)
- Blood in stools
- Signs of dehydration: dry mouth, little or no urination, dizziness
- Diarrhea lasting more than 3 days
- Symptoms in pregnant women, elderly, infants, or immunocompromised

WHEN TO GO TO EMERGENCY ROOM:
- Inability to keep any liquids down for 24 hours
- Signs of severe dehydration
- Bloody vomit or stools
- Difficulty breathing (may indicate botulism)
- Blurred or double vision (may indicate botulism)
- Muscle weakness or paralysis

HOME CARE:
- Stay hydrated — sip water, broth, or electrolyte solutions slowly
- Avoid solid foods until vomiting stops
- Gradually reintroduce bland foods: toast, rice, bananas
- Avoid dairy, fatty foods, spicy foods, alcohol, caffeine
- Rest and allow your body to recover

RISK TIER: LOW to MEDIUM — Most cases resolve at home with rest and fluids.
Seek emergency care if severely dehydrated, very high fever, or bloody stool.
