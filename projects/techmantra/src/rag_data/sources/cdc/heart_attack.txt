Source: CDC - Heart Attack Symptoms and Warning Signs
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/heartdisease/heart_attack.htm

OVERVIEW:
A heart attack occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked. 
Heart attacks are medical emergencies. Every minute without treatment 
causes more heart muscle to die. Quick action saves lives.

CLASSIC SYMPTOMS:
- Chest pain, pressure, tightness, or discomfort lasting more than a few minutes
- Pain or discomfort that spreads to the shoulder, arm, back, neck, or jaw
- Shortness of breath — may occur with or before chest discomfort
- Cold sweat
- Nausea or vomiting
- Lightheadedness or sudden dizziness
- Feeling of impending doom

SYMPTOMS IN WOMEN:
Women may experience less typical symptoms:
- Unusual fatigue lasting days before the attack
- Shortness of breath without chest pain
- Nausea and vomiting
- Back or jaw pain
- Indigestion or heartburn-like sensation
- Dizziness or lightheadedness

WHAT TO DO IMMEDIATELY:
1. Call 911 immediately — do not drive yourself
2. Chew one regular aspirin (325mg) if not allergic and not contraindicated
3. Sit or lie down and rest while waiting for help
4. Unlock the door so emergency responders can enter
5. Stay on the phone with the 911 operator

TIME IS CRITICAL:
- Treatment within 90 minutes of symptom onset saves the most heart muscle
- Clot-busting drugs or stent procedures restore blood flow
- Delay in seeking care significantly worsens outcomes and increases death risk

DO NOT:
- Wait more than 5 minutes to call 911
- Drive yourself to the hospital
- Eat or drink anything except the aspirin
- Ignore symptoms hoping they will go away

RISK TIER: HIGH — This is a life-threatening emergency. Call 911 now.
