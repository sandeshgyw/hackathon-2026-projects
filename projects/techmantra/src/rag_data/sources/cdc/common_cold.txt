Source: CDC — Common Cold
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/antibiotic-use/colds.html

The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. Antibiotics do not treat colds.

Symptoms: runny nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, mild body aches, sneezing, low-grade fever.
Duration: most colds resolve within 7–10 days.

Self-care:
- Rest and sleep
- Stay hydrated with water, broth, warm teas
- Honey (adults and children over 1 year) to soothe cough
- Saline nasal spray for congestion
- OTC pain relievers for sore throat or fever

When to see a doctor:
- Symptoms lasting more than 10 days
- High fever (above 103°F / 39.4°C)
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Severe sore throat or ear pain
