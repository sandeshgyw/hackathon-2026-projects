Source: CDC - Influenza (Flu) Symptoms
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/flu/symptoms/index.html

OVERVIEW:
Influenza (flu) is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza 
viruses. It can cause mild to severe illness and can lead to 
hospitalization or death in high-risk individuals.

SYMPTOMS:
Flu symptoms usually come on suddenly and include:
- Fever or feeling feverish and having chills (not everyone gets a fever)
- Cough — usually dry
- Sore throat
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Muscle or body aches
- Headaches
- Fatigue and weakness — often severe
- Vomiting and diarrhea — more common in children than adults

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN COLD AND FLU:
Flu symptoms are more severe and come on faster than cold symptoms.
- Fever: Common with flu, rare with cold
- Body aches: Common and severe with flu, mild with cold
- Fatigue: Severe with flu, mild with cold
- Onset: Sudden with flu, gradual with cold

WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE:
Adults — seek emergency care if you have:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent pain or pressure in chest or abdomen
- Persistent dizziness or confusion
- Severe or persistent vomiting
- Symptoms that improve then return with fever and worsening cough

Children — seek emergency care if child has:
- Fast or troubled breathing
- Bluish lips or face
- Ribs pulling in with each breath
- Chest pain
- Severe muscle pain, confusion, or seizures

TREATMENT:
- Rest and stay home to avoid spreading illness
- Drink plenty of fluids — water, broth, sports drinks
- Over the counter medications for fever and body aches
- Antiviral medications (Tamiflu) most effective if started within 48 hours
- Antibiotics do NOT work on flu — it is a virus

HOME CARE:
- Rest for at least 24 hours after fever is gone without medication
- Drink 8 to 10 glasses of fluids daily
- Use a humidifier to ease congestion
- Gargle with warm salt water for sore throat

RISK TIER: LOW to MEDIUM — Most people recover at home. 
Seek care if you are high risk or symptoms worsen significantly.
