Source: CDC - Meningitis
URL: https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/symptoms.html

OVERVIEW:
Meningitis is inflammation of the membranes surrounding the brain and 
spinal cord. Bacterial meningitis is life-threatening and requires 
immediate emergency medical care. Viral meningitis is more common and 
usually less severe.

SYMPTOMS:
- Sudden high fever (above 102F / 39C)
- Stiff neck — inability to touch chin to chest
- Severe headache that feels different from normal headaches
- Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
- Sensitivity to loud noise (phonophobia)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Seizures
- Confusion, difficulty concentrating, altered mental status
- Skin rash (in bacterial meningitis — rash does not fade under pressure)
- Sleepiness or difficulty waking up

EMERGENCY WARNING SIGNS:
The combination of stiff neck, high fever, and severe headache is a 
medical emergency. Call 911 immediately. Do not drive yourself to the 
hospital. Bacterial meningitis can cause death within 24 to 48 hours 
if untreated. Every minute counts.

Other emergency signs:
- Sudden onset of symptoms within hours
- Rash that spreads rapidly
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizures
- Difficulty breathing

RISK FACTORS:
- Age: Infants under 1 year and young adults aged 16-23 are at highest risk
- Living in close quarters: college dormitories, military barracks
- Weakened immune system: HIV, diabetes, steroid use
- Skipping meningitis vaccination
- Travel to areas with high meningitis rates

TREATMENT:
Bacterial meningitis requires immediate hospitalization. Treatment 
includes intravenous antibiotics, corticosteroids to reduce 
inflammation, and supportive care. Delay in treatment significantly 
worsens outcomes. Viral meningitis usually resolves on its own with 
rest and fluids but still requires medical evaluation.

RISK TIER: HIGH — This is a medical emergency. Call 911 immediately.
